Tools for Optimizing Sleep & Sleep-Wake Timing

Frank Efficiency 2023-08-19

Sleep is the foundation of mental and physical health and performance. Optimizing sleep can lead to better focus, energy, mood, and overall wellbeing. Here are some key tools discussed in the podcast for improving sleep quality:

Morning Routines

  • Get bright sunlight in eyes within 30-60 mins of waking to set circadian rhythm and cortisol levels. More light needed on cloudy days. Avoid sunglasses.
  • Take a cold shower (1-3 mins) or do exercise to increase core body temperature and alertness.

Daytime Routines

  • Limit caffeine intake, especially after 3-4pm.
  • Take a short nap or do yoga/meditation for relaxation, but not too late to disrupt nighttime sleep.
  • Get more sunlight exposure in late afternoon/evening. This anchors circadian rhythm for better nighttime sleep.

Evening Routines

  • Dim lights and avoid screens after 10pm, use candlelight/moonlight if needed. Bright light at night disrupts sleep.
  • Take a warm shower/bath and keep bedroom cool (65-68F) for better sleep.
  • Supplements like magnesium, apigenin, L-theanine, glycine, and myo-inositol can help sleep and relaxation before bed.

Lifestyle Factors

  • Maintain consistent sleep/wake times, including weekends.
  • Understand your temperature minimum (lowest body temp, typically 2 hours before habitual wake time) to shift circadian rhythm.

Optimizing these tools and routines can significantly improve sleep quality, daytime energy, focus and overall health.
