Gain Control of Your Free Time

Frank Thoughts 2023-08-13

Speaker: Laura Vanderkam

Summary:

  • We don’t build the lives we want by saving time. We build the lives we want, and then time saves itself.
  • Time is highly elastic - it stretches to accommodate what we choose to put into it.
  • Instead of saying “I don’t have time”, say “It’s not a priority”. This reminds us that time is a choice.
  • Figure out your priorities for the coming year - personal and professional. Break them into doable steps and schedule them first.
  • We have 168 hours in a week. Even busy people have time for what matters if they plan for it.
  • Small moments can have great power. Use bits of time for bits of joy.

Key Points:

  • Time stretches to fit our priorities. Treat priorities as the equivalent of a broken water heater emergency.
  • Clarify your key personal and professional priorities for the coming year. Break them into actionable steps.
  • Schedule your priorities first before filling your calendar.
  • We have more time than we think - look at the hours and plan meaning into them.
  • Small moments matter - use little bits of time for small joys.

Conclusion: Focus on what matters and you can build the life you want, even in limited time. Schedule priorities first. Find joy in small moments.
