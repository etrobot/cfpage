Al Gore on the Obstacles to Solving the Climate Crisis

Frank Thoughts 2023-08-13

In his TED talk, former US Vice President Al Gore discusses the obstacles to solving the climate crisis and speeding up solutions. He focuses on two main obstacles - the unrelenting opposition from the fossil fuel industry and the global financial system and subsidies for fossil fuels.

Gore argues that the fossil fuel industry has actively fought against climate solutions while falsely claiming to support them. He cites examples of oil companies like BP and ExxonMobil reversing their commitments to clean energy. He also criticizes the use of carbon capture and direct air capture as excuses to continue increasing fossil fuel production.

On the financial side, Gore points out that governments provided over $1 trillion in subsidies to the fossil fuel industry in 2021. He argues that fossil fuel companies should be prohibited from participating in climate negotiations if they do not have credible net zero plans.

However, Gore remains optimistic that we can accelerate progress once these obstacles are removed. He highlights the rapid growth of renewables, electric vehicles, battery storage and more. If we reach true net zero emissions, global temperature rise can be halted within just a few years, offering hope for the future.
