A 3-Step Guide to Believing in Yourself

Frank Thoughts 2023-08-13

At a recent TED talk, Emmy-award winning actress Sheryl Lee Ralph shared a 3-step process for believing in yourself even during challenging times.

Background

Ralph opened her talk by singing an uplifting song she had performed after winning an Emmy for her role on Abbott Elementary. She described feeling stunned and amazed to receive the honor after facing years of rejection in her acting career.

Ralph acknowledged we are living through difficult times dealing with the pandemic, political division, violence, climate change and more. This can make it hard to believe in the goodness of the world and in ourselves. Caring for our mental health is crucial, and Ralph offered strategies beyond medicine to reframe our self-belief.

The 3 Steps

Ralph outlined 3 key things we must do to believe in ourselves:

  1. See ourselves clearly. Ralph reflected on growing up as the only black child at her school and the hurtful remarks she endured. Her mother would sit her before a mirror and remind her of her value. This helped Ralph see her true self and not internalize the harmful words.

  2. Think about ourselves. When a Hollywood director questioned putting Ralph in films, she thought about his words and realized he acknowledged her talent and beauty. She left the meeting newly empowered to claim space in the industry.

  3. Act like we believe in ourselves. Ralph advocated boldly pursuing opportunities with faith in her abilities. This mindset led to breakthroughs like being cast on Designing Women. When we believe in ourselves and act on it, anything becomes possible.

Conclusion

In closing, Ralph challenged the audience to practice self-love, respect, encouragement and empowerment. Our relationship with ourselves is the most important one. By believing in ourselves, even seemingly broken dreams can take flight. We must have faith in our worth and never give up on ourselves.
