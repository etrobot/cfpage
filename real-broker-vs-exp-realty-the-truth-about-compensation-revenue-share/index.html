Real Broker vs eXp Realty - The Truth About Compensation & Revenue Share

Overview

This post provides an in-depth comparison of the compensation structures and revenue share models of Real Broker and eXp Realty. It analyzes the pros and cons of each brokerage and looks at long-term implications for agents.

Key Points

  • eXp has grown much faster than Real Broker since it was founded (78,000 agents vs 4,000 agents in the same timeframe)
  • The compensation structure seems more favorable for Real at first glance, but has concerning implications long-term
  • eXp uses a hybrid revenue share model where you get some share even if you don’t fully qualify. Real uses a qualification model where you get $0 if you don’t qualify.
  • Real’s highest revenue share % is on the smallest tier, and lowest % on the biggest tier. eXp is the opposite.
  • At Real you must produce a certain amount each year to keep getting your share. At eXp you keep it for life as long as licensed.
  • eXp allows sponsors to provide additional value to new agents. Real prohibits this.
  • Concerns around Real’s ownership structure and decision making process favoring shareholders over agents.
  • Real incentivized top agents to join. eXp grows organically.
  • eXp seems more innovative with cloud brokerage vs Real’s mobile app.

Recommendations

  • Real better for low producing agents who just want to save on fees
  • eXp better for productive agents who want exit strategy, and long term residual income
  • Do thorough research before choosing, don’t rely on emotions or assumptions
