How I Gained 30k Instagram Followers with Reels

Over the past few months, I’ve grown my Instagram following from 35k to over 66k, and it’s all thanks to Reels.

Unlike what most Instagram tips videos recommend, I don’t rely on trending audios, hashtags, or posting times. My strategy focuses more on creating quality, original content that provides value to my audience.

There are two main types of Reels that work well:

Trendy Reels

These use popular audios, effects, and hashtags to jump on trends. They’re usually only 7 seconds long.

Original Reels

I like to think of these as mini YouTube videos tailored for Reels. I share tips, behind-the-scenes info, and other valuable content.

The key is spending time brainstorming engaging ideas rather than optimizing small details like hashtags. To go viral, make content that is:

  • Taboo - Talk about unpopular opinions or topics people avoid

  • Timely - Reference trending news, memes, or pop culture

  • Tangible Tips - Provide actionable advice people can implement

For filming, I use a Sony camera, tripod, and microphone to get high-quality shots efficiently. I edit in Premiere Pro to have more control.

For trendy Reels made with phone clips, I import to Adobe Rush then to Premiere to avoid issues with iPhone footage.

Once edited, I add music and captions in the Instagram app. I use my YouTube thumbnails as the Reels covers for branding.

The most important thing is spending time ideating engaging concepts over optimizing small details. This focus on quality content is how I’ve grown my following so quickly with Reels.
